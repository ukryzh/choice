Case study

How choice was built — from a market gap in FemTech to a working privacy-first MVP, designed and shipped solo.

Problem

Most cycle trackers monetize the most intimate kind of personal data — selling, sharing, or analyzing it. Users have no real opt-out.

Insight

Privacy is usually declared, not enforced. Users compromise because there is no real alternative — not because they don't care.

Approach

Privacy by Design: local-first storage, per-record AES-256 encryption, no analytics, no ads, opt-in encrypted cloud backups.

Outcome

An Android MVP with cycle logging, history, predictions, symptoms, encrypted backups, and cross-device data recovery.

Process

1. Market research

Audit of Flo, Clue, Period Tracker, WomanLog, Maya privacy policies. Confirmed market gap: no mainstream cycle tracker treats privacy as a hard constraint.

2. Scope & vision

MVP defined as: cycle & symptom tracking, history, predictions, encrypted cloud backup, recovery on new device. Monetization, analytics and clinical claims explicitly out of scope.

3. Architecture

Client–server split where the server only ever sees encrypted blobs. Per-record AES-256 encryption. Encryption key derived on the device and never uploaded.

4. Implementation

Android client with local SQLite/Room storage. Firebase used as a "blind" backend — encrypted JSON only. Optional Google sign-in is for cloud backup identity, not data access.

5. Compliance

GDPR-aligned by construction: minimal data, user-controlled deletion, transparent privacy story, no third-party data sharing.

6. Next

Beta testing with real users, UX polish, prediction tuning, and Google Play release.
