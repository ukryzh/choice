Market Research Document

1. Research Objective

Primary Objective

To evaluate the feasibility of developing a menstrual cycle tracking application with a strong emphasis on user data privacy.

Research Questions

  1. Is there user demand for privacy-first solutions in the cycle tracking category?
  2. How do current market leaders handle user data?
  3. Is there a market gap between user expectations and existing products?
  4. What constraints does a privacy-first approach impose on the product and business model?

2. Scope and Limitations

In Scope

  • Mobile cycle tracking applications
  • Android / iOS consumer market
  • Privacy, data handling, monetization models
  • Qualitative and desk research

Out of Scope

  • UX/UI comparative analysis
  • Clinical accuracy of cycle predictions
  • Enterprise / B2B healthcare solutions
  • Regulatory deep dive (GDPR / medical compliance — high-level only)

4. User Problem Definition

Core Problem

Users are forced to share intimate medical data in exchange for access to basic app functionality.

Types of Collected Data

  • Menstrual cycle dates
  • Pregnancy and fertility data
  • Symptoms, mood, sexual activity (in some apps)
  • Device and behavioral metadata

Identified Risks

  • Use of data for targeted advertising
  • Potential sharing with third parties
  • Legal and social risks (especially regarding reproductive rights)

5. Competitive Analysis (High-Level)

Monetization & Data Practices

Analysis of privacy policies of popular apps reveals:

  • Collection of personal and sensitive data
  • Sharing data with third parties (analytics, ads)
  • Use of aggregated data for research
  • Vague language about “service improvement”

Key Insight: privacy is often declared rather than genuinely enforced.

  • Flo Privacy Policy
  • Period Tracker Privacy Policy
  • Clue Privacy Policy
  • WomanLog Privacy Policy

7. Privacy Paradox Explanation

Observed Behavior

  • Users continue using apps with questionable privacy practices
  • Privacy policies are rarely read
  • Opt-out mechanisms are complex or missing

Conclusion

User behavior reflects not indifference, but high switching costs, lack of alternatives, and cognitive overload.

8. Identified Market Gap

Gap Definition

No product currently offers:

  • No third-party data sharing
  • Local data storage
  • No advertising
  • No mandatory subscription

Target User Segment

  • Privacy-conscious users
  • Technically curious users
  • Users willing to trade convenience for control
  • Self-quantification enthusiasts

Note: this is a niche, not mass-market segment.

10. Strategic Conclusion

  • The problem exists
  • A market gap exists
  • Demand exists (niche)
  • Traditional business models do not fit

11. Final Decision

The project will:

  • Avoid aggressive monetization
  • Focus on privacy-first architecture
  • Be treated as a product experiment, not a startup

12. Open Questions

  • Long-term sustainability without monetization
  • Legal resilience of privacy-first solutions
  • Potential for community-driven development
