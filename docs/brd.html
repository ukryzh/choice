Business Requirements Document (BRD)

1. Document Overview

1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this document is to define the business requirements for a mobile application for menstrual cycle tracking, with a strong focus on user data privacy and secure data transfer between devices.

1.2 Scope of Document

This document describes:

  • product business goals
  • key business requirements
  • constraints and risks
  • success criteria

Technical implementation details and system architecture are out of scope for this document.

2. Business Background

2.1 Problem Statement

Most menstrual cycle tracking applications on the market are monetized through:

  • collection and analysis of personal data
  • data sharing with third parties
  • use of user data for advertising and research purposes

Users lack a secure alternative that allows them to fully control their data and be confident in its confidentiality.

2.2 Business Opportunity

Growing user awareness of privacy concerns and advances in AI tools make it possible to create:

  • a privacy-first product
  • without dependence on data sales
  • with minimal infrastructure
  • focused on user trust rather than monetization

3. Business Objectives

3.1 Primary Objectives

  • Provide users with a secure menstrual cycle tracking application
  • Ensure full user control over personal data
  • Eliminate selling or analyzing user data

3.2 Secondary Objectives

  • Enable data transfer between devices
  • Minimize regulatory and legal risks

4. Stakeholders

Stakeholder Role
Product Owner Defines vision and priorities
Developer Product implementation
Regulatory Bodies Compliance oversight
End Users Use of the product

5. Target Audience

5.1 Primary Users

  • Women before menopause
  • Pregnant women

5.2 User Needs

  • Simple and clear cycle tracking
  • Confidential handling of medical data
  • Ability to restore data when switching devices
  • No ads or hidden data collection

6. Business Requirements

6.1 Core Business Requirements

  • The app must be usable without registration
  • User data must be stored locally on the device
  • Optional encrypted cloud backup must be available
  • User data must not be used for analytics, advertising, or research
  • Users must be able to delete all their data

6.2 Data Privacy & Compliance Requirements

  • All user data must be encrypted on the client side
  • The server must not have access to decrypted data
  • The application must comply with GDPR
  • The architecture must follow Privacy by Design and Privacy by Default principles
  • Explicit user consent is required for cloud storage

6.3 Platform Requirements

  • Supported platform: Android
  • Compliance with Google Play health app requirements

7. Out of Scope (Business Perspective)

  • Monetization
  • Integration with medical institutions
  • User data analysis
  • Advertising and research features
  • Medical recommendations

8. Assumptions

  • Users value data privacy
  • Users are willing to use cloud storage if end-to-end encryption is provided
  • Users understand the value of data backups
  • The product is developed and maintained by a single person

9. Constraints

  • Limited financial resources
  • Limited human resources
  • Compliance with GDPR and Google Play requirements
  • Secure server infrastructure limitations

10. Risks and Mitigation

Risk Impact Mitigation
Regulatory restrictions High Privacy-first architecture, E2E encryption
User distrust High Transparent privacy policy
Limited resources Medium Clearly defined MVP
Prediction errors Medium No medical recommendations

11. Success Criteria

11.1 Business Success Metrics

  • The app is published on Google Play
  • No violations related to personal data processing
  • Users can securely transfer data between devices

11.2 Acceptance Criteria

  • Users can start using the app without registration
  • Users can create, read, update, delete, and download their data
  • Users fully control cloud storage usage
  • The developer has no access to user data
